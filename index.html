About Me

I'm a highly-motivated electrical engineering student who loves finding ways to apply classroom knowledge. I'm particularly interested in projects that relate to aerospace and aviation. In EPICS, I worked on designing a Mars rover and in high school I did a project about Mars chemistry and the Curiosity rover. I hope to translate this into my professional career through internships and a full-time job after I graduate for a company who leads in the aerospace or space exploration fields.

Outside of the classroom I try to apply my electrical engineering skills to small musical projects like creating synthesizers or guitar pedals. I've made light shows where each light corresponds to a note on the musical scale. This interest stems from playing guitar and piano in my spare time. I also love outdoor activities such as camping and biking.

    Password protected thermostat, LCD display

    Spring '15

    This project uses an Arduino mega with a temperature sensor, buzzer, tri color LED, LED state indicators, a control pad, and LCD screen. You must enter the correct password on the control pad to obtain access to temperature and the amount of time that the system has been on. The LEDs indicate which mode you are in and the buzzer beeps for every button press. The tri color led quickly rotates between colors just for fun!
